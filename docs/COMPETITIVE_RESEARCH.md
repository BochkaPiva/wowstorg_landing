# Competitive Research

## What strong event and experiential agencies usually show

### Global references

- Jack Morton emphasizes services, work, insights, geography, and case-study storytelling. Its work page frames projects through strategy, creative, innovation, and execution.
- GPJ positions itself around scalable experiential portfolios, strategy, experience design, production, logistics, fabrication, and analytics.
- Set Creative leads with creative experiential positioning, then work, insights, about, careers, and contact.

### Russian references

- Eventum Premo uses a classic agency structure: info, services, projects, team, news, order project, contacts.
- Redday leans into showreel, business tasks, team scale, awards/ranking, and cases.
- Big Jack exposes service categories directly: тимбилдинги, деловые мероприятия, маркетинговые мероприятия, корпоративы, awards, cases, and lead CTA.
- BE BRAND separates case filters by event type: corporate, business, mass, and marketing events.

## Useful patterns for WOWSTORG

1. Lead with a cinematic mascot-driven brand moment, not a generic “full-cycle agency” claim.
2. Move quickly from emotion to selection: formats, catalog, scenarios.
3. Make catalog a conversion engine, not a shop grid: every item should answer “where it works”, “for whom”, “what effect it creates”.
4. Use cases as proof, but avoid invented client names until real data exists.
5. Add process only after desire is created; process should reduce risk, not become the main story.
6. Use FAQ for SEO and conversion objections.
7. Keep one persistent CTA intent: describe the task and receive a fitting format.

## What to avoid

- huge stats without evidence;
- fake clients and logo walls;
- generic “services / portfolio / contacts” navigation without a narrative;
- too many equal cards;
- catalog hidden too late on the page;
- “full cycle” as the main message before the user understands the value.
